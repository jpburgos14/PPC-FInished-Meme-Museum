Social Memes

  • 2000s-2010s

    Aldub

    Aldub, a popular love team from the show Eat Bulaga, became a national sensation in the 2010s. They created numerous trends like the "Pabebe Wave," captivating audiences and dominating online discussions.

  • Present

    Malupiton

    Malupiton, a content creator, has revived the use of the word "Boss" through his skits and online presence. He has effectively become a meme himself, embodying the humor and trends of the current social media landscape.

  • Future

    Digital Nomad

    As remote work and digital nomadism rise, memes may capture the unique challenges and humorous situations faced by those who work from anywhere. This is especially relevant in the Philippines, with its scenic beauty and occasional infrastructure challenges.

Environmental Memes

  • 2000s-2010s

    Typhoon Ondoy

    This image of Typhoon Ondoy became a meme, highlighting the resilience of Filipinos in overcoming the devastating effects of the storm.

  • Present

    The Philippines: Where Even Shampoo comes in a tiny plastic packet.

    This meme, originating from a Rappler Facebook post, highlights the prevalence of single-use plastics and sachets in the Philippines. It has sparked discussions about environmental impact and waste reduction.

  • Future

    Climate Change is Real: #NewNormal

    This meme reflects concerns about the potential consequences of climate change in the Philippines, where rising sea levels could lead to submerged streets and a "new normal" for Filipino roads.

Culture Memes

  • 2000s-2010s

    Jeprox Style

    The "Jeprox" style, associated with hip-hop music and culture, gained popularity in the Philippines as a form of rebellious self-expression. It reflected the influence of Western trends and the desire of Filipino youth to express their identity through fashion and music.

  • Present

    Boy Dila

    This meme revolves around an incident during the Wattah Wattah Festival in San Juan City, where a content creator known as "Boy Dila" sprayed a passing motorcyclist with a water gun. The incident, captured on video, sparked online debate about public behavior and accountability.

  • Future

    Culture Just a Nostalgia?

    This meme expresses concerns about the potential fading of traditional Filipino cultural beliefs and practices in the future, suggesting they might become mere remnants of the past.

×

Economy Memes

  • 2000s-2010s

    Load Card: Text & Calls Promo

    While not initially a meme, prepaid load cards for texting and calling have become a nostalgic symbol of the 2000s-2010s in the Philippines, representing a time when texting was a dominant form of communication.

  • Present

    RC Cola Commercial

    RC Cola's unique and quirky commercial, featuring a family with drinking glasses on their backs and RC Cola bottles for heads, has become a popular meme, praised for its creativity and humor.

  • Future

    VR the Future?

    This meme envisions a future where Filipinos have widely adopted virtual reality technology, potentially highlighting the country's embrace of technological advancements.

×

Politics Memes

  • 2000s-2010s

    Hello Garci

    The "Hello Garci" scandal, involving alleged election rigging by former President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo, became a source of widespread memes and political satire in the Philippines.

  • Present

    P***** Marcoses & Romualdez

    This meme references recent political controversies involving Vice President Sara Duterte, former President Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr., and First Lady Liza Romualdez, highlighting ongoing political tensions and discussions.

  • Future

    The Senate Full of Artist

    This meme satirizes the possibility of a future Philippine Senate dominated by former artists, potentially commenting on the intersection of entertainment and politics.

Other Memes

  • 2000s-2010s

    Jejemon Typings

    "Jejemon" typings, characterized by excessive use of letters and symbols, emerged as a unique form of online communication in the Philippines, reflecting a playful approach to language and expression.

  • Present

    #Danas

    The "#Danas" meme expresses empathy and shared struggles among Filipinos, finding humor and comfort in collective experiences and hardships.

  • Future

    The era of Skibidi?

    This meme speculates on the potential influence of current internet trends, such as the "Skibidi" meme, on future generations and online humor.
